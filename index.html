Our Purpose

Our Mission

The Manchuro Climate Accord aims to unite micronations and micronational organizations worldwide in the fight against climate change through education, advocacy, and collaborative action.

Global Unity

Bringing together micronations and organizations from across the globe to form a united front against climate change.

Education

Educating citizens and leaders about climate science, sustainable practices, and effective climate policies.

Grassroots Action

Empowering local communities to implement climate solutions that can scale to regional, national, and international levels.

Who We Are

About Us

The Manchuro Climate Accord is the world's leading intermicronational organization focused on environmentalism. It was founded on April 21, 2025, and as of May 2025, it consists of 20 member states and 2 observer states.

Founding Date

April 21, 2025

Member States

20 member states and 2 observer states

Our Vision

To create a coalition of micronations united by shared environmental values, demonstrating that even small entities can contribute to global climate solutions.

Our Name

Etymology

M
CA

The organization's name is derived from its location in Manchuro, Ablakistan, a city within the New York Federation. The "Climate Accord" part of the name is inspired by the "Paris Climate Accord".

Manchuro

Named after the founding city in Ablakistan, where the accord was first signed by member micronations.

Climate Accord

Inspired by international climate agreements, emphasizing our commitment to environmental collaboration.

The Stakes Are High

Every day of inaction brings us closer to irreversible tipping points in our climate system. We must act now to secure a livable planet for future generations.

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures continue to rise at an alarming rate, resulting in extreme weather events.

Sea Level Rise

Rising sea levels threaten coastal communities and island nations worldwide.

Biodiversity Loss

Species are going extinct at unprecedented rates due to habitat destruction and climate change.
